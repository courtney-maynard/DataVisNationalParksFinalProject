Compare Features Of Different National Parks

How To Use This Visualization Tool

This visualization demonstrates the features you could encounter/experience in each national park. These features were defined by the National Parks Service as well as All Trails.

  • Hover over any rectangle to see: name of feature, number of trails that have this feature associated with them, average popularity of trails with this feature, and top ~30 trails alphabetically with the specific feature
  • Use the drop down menus or sliders to change which national park is showing
  • Larger rectangles indicate more trails with that feature
  • Darker rectangles indicate more popular features, as computed by the average popularity across all trails with that activity

Possible Features: Accessibility-Friendly, Beach, Cave, Child-Friendly, City Walk, Dogs Allowed, Dogs Must Be Leashed, Dogs Prohibited, Forest, Fully Paved, Historic Site, Hot Springs, Lake, Partially Paved, Rails to Trails Conversion, River, Scenic Views, Strollers Allowed, Waterfall, Wild Flowers, Wildlife



Note On Trail Popularity:

The most popular features differ across parks because all parks have different terrain and natural features, as well as different 'highlights' or unique features only found in that park, such as geothermal features in Yellowstone! However, for most parks, trails that were accessibility-friendly, child friendly, and fully or partially paved have the highest average popularity. .

Additionally, the average popularity of trails with common features, such as scenic views, may be lower or average simply because there are many trails with that attribute.
